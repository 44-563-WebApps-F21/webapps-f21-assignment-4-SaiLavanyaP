An Ideal Husband

Morning-room at Sir Robert Chiltern’s house.

[LORD GORING _is standing by the fireplace with his hands in his pockets_. He is looking rather bored.]


LORD GORING.

Thanks awfully, but I think I’d sooner be engaged before lunch.

LORD CAVERSHAM.

Humph! Never know when you are serious or not.

LORD GORING.

Neither do I, father.

[A pause.]

LORD CAVERSHAM.

I suppose you have read The Times this morning?

LORD GORING.

[Airily.]

The Times? Certainly not. I only read _The Morning Post_. All that one should know about modern life is where the Duchesses are; anything else is quite demoralising.
